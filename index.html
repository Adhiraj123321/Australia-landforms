What are Landforms?

  • A landform is a naturally-formed feature on the Earth's surface, often with a recognizable shape like a valley or mountain. They range in size and can be small like hills or much larger like mountains.

  • Recherche Archipelago

    An archipelago is a group of Islands. The biggest archepelego that is located in Australia is called the Recherche Archipelago. Locally, it is know as the bay of Isles. It consists of 150 islands.The islands strech 230 km from east to west.

Peninsula's of Australia

  • Eyre Peninsula

    A peninsula is a landform that is mostly, but not completely, surrounded by water. The main peninsula in Australia is the Eyre Peninsula. It was named after Edward John Eyre.This piece of land projects into the India Ocean. It has a broad base, giving it a triangular formation.

Deserts of Australia

  • Great Victoria Desert

    A desert is where living conditions are hostile for plant and animal life.There are 10 deserts in Australia. The biggest one is called Great Victoria Desert. It is over 700 km (430 mi) wide (from west to east) and covers an area of 348,750 km2.

Mountains of Australia

  • Mount Kosciuszko

    A mountain is a large natural elevation of the earth's surface rising abruptly from the surrounding level. Mountains cover a large part of Australia. The highest mountain is called Mount Kosciuszko. It has a elevation of 2,228 m. It is located in the state of New South Wales, in the southeastern part of the country.

Canyon's of Australia

  • Kings Canoyon

    A canyon is a deep gorge, typically with a river flowing through it. Australia has more than 750 canyons. The largest is the Kings Canyon. It's maximum depth is 8,200 feet. It is approximately 3,300 feet (1,000 meters) long. Because of these features, Kings Canyon is leviethan in size.

Ranges of Australia

  • The Great Dividing Range

    A range is a chain of hills or mountains. The Great Dividing Range, also known as the Eastern Highlands, is Australia's most extensive mountain range, stretching more than 3,500 kilometers (2,175 miles).

Rainforests of Australia

  • Daintree Rainforest

    The Daintree Rainforest is a region on the northeast coast of Queensland, Australia. At around 1,200 square kilometer, the Daintree is a part of the largest continuous area of tropical rainforest on the Australian continent. Along the coastline north of the Daintree River, tropical forest grows right down to the edge of the sea.
